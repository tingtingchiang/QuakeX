How will you combat an Earthquake?

According to USGS statistics, about every 30 seconds, an earthquake occurs somewhere in the world. In fact, they estimate that "several million earthquakes occur in the world each year".

Will you be near one?

Of course, not everyone lives near a known fault line, but it's good to be prepared. After all, when there isn't an earthquake, everything might seem fine, perfect even. But when that quake strikes, you're going to be suprised.

It might even be a - shock.

In those few precious seconds before and after an earthquake strikes, you need to be on your toes. Those critical moments could mark how prepared you are for the future.

But what do you do?

LOCATION

Where you are in relation to known fault lines affects how much urgent earthquake preparedness is. And when an earthquake does happen in your area, where you are in relation to the epicenter is a huge factor between hiding and fleeing. Being further away from the center increases the time you have before the shock hits you.

Where will you be safe?

PREPARATION

Imagine. Seconds before the earthquake strikes, you get an alert. You're surprised, sure, but now you have a few seconds to get under cover, gather family members, or collect belongings. What do you target first? Do you even know where to find them?

What are you priorities?

TRANSPORTATION

Let's assume you make it through a major earthquake. Next steps would include moving out of impacted areas to safety. Your first instinct may be to get in your car and drive down the road. Even if the car hasn't been damaged, you've got problems ahead of you. It seems like everyone else has the same idea, the roads are congested like the LA highway during rush hour. Besides, the government needs the roads relatively cleared so they can begin their own emergency procedures.

How do you get to safety?

RESOURCES

You made it out alive, but do you have everything you need? Water, food, extra clothing, toiletries, emergency cash? Probably not, meaning you'll need to purchase them. Without cash, your only solution is to access your funds from your bank. But at this time, banks are most likely crowded by others in the same situation as you. There's no way you can get the supplies you need in time.

Where do you go?

Too many questions, not enough time. You've only got a few seconds during an earthquake.

QuakeX's Significant Features

Designed to focus on your priorities and your needs, QuakeX provides a comprehensive overview of what you should be doing before, during, and after an earthquake. Not the population of humans on earth, just you. See how by clicking on the various components.


Login Screen: Create an account with details about your living conditions such as house type and number of household members. Based on Parse Backend

Procedure Prepration: From the US government and other professional organizations, we have compiled an extensive list on how users should prepare and react to earthquake happenings. Users will only receive tips that are relevant to them, based on their location, household details, and other information they provided in the start.

Earthquake Map: Drawing data from the US Geographical Survey Department, QuakeX creates maps that show an area's earthquake severity and probable occurence. Users can then use this survey to determine how much they should prepare beforehand.

Safe Zone Travel: Users can use QuakeX to find the best and safe location based on the earthquake epicenter and their current location. Addtionally, users can connect to the Uber service to find rides going to the same area.
